AP Language FRQ Final Reminder

Why you might be stuck on 4/6?

Most students do not lose points because they have no ideas. They lose points because they do not make their argument clear enough, explain their evidence deeply enough, or keep linking everything back to the thesis.

Thesis Paragraphs Evidence Sophistication Final Check
4/6
You are probably describing more than arguing. The jump happens in the commentary.
⚠️

The biggest reason students stay at 4/6

They include evidence, but they do not clearly explain how that evidence proves the claim and connects back to the thesis.

Your examiner should never have to think: “What is the point of this evidence?” You must explain it for them.
🎯

1. Thesis: take a clear position

Your thesis does not need to be beautiful. It needs to be clear, specific, and arguable.

  • Answer the prompt directly.
  • Take a side or make a clear judgement.
  • Give the essay a direction.
  • Avoid “there are positives and negatives” unless you clearly state your final position.
Weak thesis

“This issue has advantages and disadvantages.”

Stronger thesis

“Although the policy may create short-term challenges, schools should adopt it because it improves student habits and forces communities to think more responsibly about health and sustainability.”

🧱

2. Paragraphs: use Claim → Evidence → Commentary

A body paragraph is not a place to dump everything you know. It is a mini-argument that proves one part of your thesis.

Claim What is this paragraph proving?
→
Evidence What example, source detail, or quote proves it?
→
Commentary How and why does it prove your point?
The missing move: link the paragraph back to your thesis. Do not assume the connection is obvious.
🔍

3. Evidence: explain HOW and WHY

The best essays explain evidence. They do not drop in a quote and move on.

After every piece of evidence, write commentary that answers these questions:

✓ What does the evidence show?
✓ How does it support the paragraph’s claim?
✓ How does it connect back to your thesis?
✓ Why does it matter?
💬

Description is not enough

Everybody can describe what a source says. Higher-scoring essays explain what the evidence means inside the argument.

Quote drop

“Source B says students are affected by cafeteria choices. This shows schools should change their menus.”

Explained evidence

“Source B suggests that cafeteria choices shape students’ daily habits. This matters because schools are not only feeding students once; they are repeatedly normalizing certain choices. If the thesis argues that schools should promote healthier habits, this evidence supports that claim by showing that cafeteria policy can influence behavior over time.”

🔗

Link back

End paragraphs by reconnecting to the main argument.

  • This proves my thesis because...
  • This matters because...
  • Therefore, the issue is really about...
🚫

Avoid this

  • Starting paragraphs with “Source A says...”
  • Using evidence without explanation.
  • Repeating the same idea in every paragraph.
  • Ending a paragraph right after a quote.
🧠

Think like this

Do not ask, “Did I include evidence?”

Ask: “Did I teach the reader how to understand my evidence?”
✨

4. To get the extra sophistication, do one or more of these

Sophistication is not about using fancy words. It is about showing deeper thinking across the essay.

Acknowledge the seriousness of the counter-argument

Do not write a lazy counterargument like “Some people disagree.” Take the opposing view seriously, explain why it is reasonable, and then show why it is not strong enough to change your mind.

Move

“Critics are right to worry that this policy may create practical problems. However, that concern does not outweigh the long-term benefits because...”

Explain the limitations of your argument

Show that you understand your argument is not perfect. Do this in more than one paragraph if possible, not just in one quick sentence at the end.

Move

“This solution would not work equally well in every community, especially where resources are limited. Still, that limitation means implementation must be flexible, not that the idea should be rejected.”

Show why the issue matters in wider or different contexts

Connect the issue to a broader consequence: society, education, public health, economics, ethics, technology, democracy, identity, or the future.

Move

“The debate is not only about one policy. It reveals a larger question about what responsibilities schools have when shaping the habits and values of young people.”

✅

Before you submit

  • My thesis takes a clear position.
  • Each paragraph has one clear claim.
  • My evidence is specific.
  • I explain how my evidence supports the claim.
  • I connect my evidence back to the thesis.
  • I explain why the point matters.
  • I show complexity somewhere in the essay.
Final rule: Do not just prove you understood the topic. Prove that you can build an argument about it.
